OHSAS ISO 45001


What is OHSAS ISO 45001?


Over 2.3 million people die every year due to work related accidents. ISO45001 has been designed to help organizations deal with Occupational Health and Safety (OH &S) Management Systems.


This Standard provides a framework to enhance and fortify employee safety, reduce workplace risks and create safer, healthier working conditions.


It incorporates the generic approaches of the ISO 14001 and ISO 9001, OHSAS18001 Guidelines and conventions outlined by the International Labour Organization and various national standards relevant to this area of operation.


Who Needs OHSAS ISO 45001?


Risk to health and safety is a universal occupational hazard. This Standard is applicable to any organization regardless of its size, type and nature- micro business or a global conglomerate, non-profit organization, a charity, an academic institution, or a government department.


    Implementation of this Standard


  • Identify and develop OH&S policy and OH&S objectives.


  • Establish systematic processes on the basis of risks and opportunities- legal and other requirements.


  • Increase awareness of OH&S risks.


  • Organizations using ISO 9001 can migrate to ISO 9001:2015.


  • It applies to any organization, regardless of size or industry.


  • Organizations using ISO 9001 can migrate to ISO 9001:2015.


    • Advantage OHSAS ISO 45001


  • Enables efficient management of OH & S risks, prevents accidents/injury/ill-health and improves performance.


  • Reduces absenteeism and employee turnover rates.


  • Reduces incidence costs, downtime and disruption costs.


  • Reduce cost of insurance premiums.


  • Improves emergency preparedness and response.


  • Supports sustainability initiatives by ensuring health and safety of employees and the communities around.


  • Ensures accountability of top management and demonstrates commitment through leadership.


  • Recognizes the value of worker involvement and worker consultations to develop and apply better OHS practices.


    • What is OHSAS 18001?


    Occupational Health and Safety Assessment Series or OHSAS, (officially BS OHSAS 18001) is an internationally applied British Standard for occupational health and safety management systems.


    This is the world’s first occupational health and safety international standard that helps organizations provide a safe and healthy workplace for workers/employees, prevent deaths, work-related injury and ill-health as well as continually improves performance.


    This Standard was recently updated & adopted as OHSAS 18001:2007 to provide greater emphasis on ‘Heath’ rather than just ‘Safety’.


    Requirements


    This standard requires an organization to formally document the areas that are covered by their OH&S policy and maintain evidence that it is operating according to the requirements of the standard.


    How to Create this Standard


  • Write an OH&S policy authorized by top management, appropriate to the organization, identifying health and safety risks. This must be communicated to all stakeholders to ensure a continuous improvement process and review.


  • Create a system for risk management to identify health and safety risks and event control systems to minimize the effects. This should also identify training needs and operational controls.


  • Identify relevant legislation and requirements pertaining to health and safety.


  • Set Objectives to reduce or eliminate risks and to comply with the relevant legislation.


  • Develop and implement systems to achieve the objectives: The systems should be regularly reviewed and modified when required.


  • Monitor and measure: The performance of the OH&S Management System must be monitored and measured through a program of audits, analysis of events, non-conformances and preventative actions and of progress versus the objectives.


    • Combination of Standard


    OHSAS 18001 can be aligned with existing ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 Management Systems. Historically, many organizations start with the Quality Management System ISO 9001, and then add the environment management requirements from ISO 14001.


    This Standard is compatible with ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 Management System Standards.


    Advantage OHSAS 18001


    This Standard provides a framework that helps organizations to:


  • Identify and check health and safety risks


  • Minimize accidents/ill health and death


  • Ensure legal compliance


  • Enhance overall performance


  • Reduction in insurance premiums and penalties for breach of legislation.


  • Demonstrate corporate responsibilities and meet supply chain requirements.


  • Protect brand reputation


  • Motivate and engage staff through consultation and participation
